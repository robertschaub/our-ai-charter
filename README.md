# Our AI Charter

**A charter for trustworthy AI — open, plural, and accountable to people.**

_Early public draft — open for comment. Not a certification; not yet a standard._

AI is becoming the infrastructure the world learns, decides, and creates with. **Our AI Charter** is a public-interest effort to keep that infrastructure trustworthy:

- **Purpose-bound** — assessed systems state what they are for, what they must not be used for, who may be affected, and what risks are known;
- **Answerable to people** — a named human institution is responsible, and consequential uses have notice, review, challenge, and remedy;
- **Safe, secure, private, and resilient** — systems protect people, data, infrastructure, continuity, and public-interest reliance;
- **Fair in practice** — materially uneven or discriminatory performance is tested and monitored where relevant, with limits disclosed and credible harms escalated, mitigated, corrected, constrained, or withdrawn where necessary;
- **Open to evidence and correction** — material claims, sources where used, limits, incidents, changes, drift, complaints, corrections, withdrawals, and unresolved risks leave enough evidence for appropriate review and action.

Website: **[robertschaub.github.io/our-ai-charter](https://robertschaub.github.io/our-ai-charter/)** · Program map: **[PROGRAM.md](PROGRAM.md)**.

## One goal

**The goal:** keep advanced AI a *trustworthy, public, and accountable* foundation for free societies — open enough to inspect, governed well enough to trust, and stewarded as shared infrastructure rather than owned and switched off by a few. Our AI Charter pursues it through five public obligations, a three-layer assurance stack, and a first evaluation protocol, asking of any deployed system: *can it show its work and answer for it?*

This is one strand of a broader program — *building a fair, stable, and sustainable society that uses new technology responsibly* ([program map](PROGRAM.md)). Our AI Charter is stewarded by Robert Schaub and the **FactHarbor Verein**.

## Status

**Phase 1 — public drafting.** The work so far is documentary: published articles, a worked-out evaluation method, and the connections to take it further. **Nothing is certified yet** — there is no Trust Mark, certificate, accredited assessor, or certification body, and independent pilot evaluations are a possible next step, not yet underway. A real conformity scheme (mark, registry, assessor accreditation) would be Phase 2, only if pilots and demand justify it — built *inside* existing bodies, not as a new silo. Everything outside *Published* is an early draft, released to be argued with.

## How to help

This can't be built by one person. Three concrete asks — open an issue:

1. **Break it** — where does the thinking or the method fail or mislead?
2. **Co-design the measurement** — help set a defensible evaluation design for one use-case.
3. **Pilot it** — volunteer a system for an exploratory trial evaluation; no mark is issued.

See **[CONTRIBUTING](CONTRIBUTING.md)** for the working norms (open drafts, published dissent, conflict-of-interest disclosure).

For suspected leaked secrets, private material, or personal data, do **not** open a public issue. Contact **[info@factharbor.ch](mailto:info@factharbor.ch)** or follow **[SECURITY](SECURITY.md)**.
