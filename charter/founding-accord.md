# Our AI Charter — the Founding Accord
### A public charter for trustworthy AI — built together, answerable to people.

_Draft v0.2 (2026-06-13). Open for comment._

---

**Preamble**

Artificial intelligence has become critical infrastructure for how the world learns, decides, and creates. Too much of it now depends on a few providers, in a few jurisdictions, whose offerings can be withdrawn, constrained, or redirected by forces no user controls. The answer is not retreat into rival camps, but cooperation — a shared, trustworthy foundation for AI that is open, plural, secure, and answerable to people. A network, not a fortress.

We commit to building that foundation together, and to being held to the values and commitments below.

**Our values**

- **Humanity** — we serve people and the common good first.
- **Justice** — power over AI must be accountable and fairly shared.
- **Respect** — we protect autonomy, privacy, consent, and the plurality of the world's peoples and views.
- **Truth** — we are honest, transparent, and verifiable, and we stand against manipulation.
- **Solidarity** — we build together, support one another, and steward the shared commons.

**Three ways to take part**

- **Supporter** — any person or institution may publicly endorse this Accord.
- **Member** — organisations that join the Charter, accept its shared, capture-resistant governance, and commit to the path below.
- **Trust-Mark holder** — members whose specific model or system has passed independent audit against the commitments. The Mark is granted **per model and version — never as a blanket badge for an organisation** — and may be revoked.

**Our commitments** (to earn and keep the Trust Mark for a given model or system, under independent audit):

1. **Independent safety evaluation.** Third-party safety and robustness checks before release. We do not grade our own homework.
2. **Auditable transparency, ethically sourced.** We disclose, in verifiable form, our training-data sources, known limitations, and incidents — and we source data lawfully and with consent. Transparency about wrongdoing does not excuse it.
3. **Privacy and consent by default.** Lawful, clear data handling; no covert surveillance or non-consensual use of people's data.
4. **Fair, capture-resistant participation.** We uphold the federation's anti-domination rules, take no more than our fair share of control, and accept that the Mark can be withdrawn for breach. _(This governs conduct within the federation; it does not require members to restructure their own companies.)_

We further strive toward open and verifiable models, interoperability and real exit rights, security by design, and giving back to the commons.

**Independent and fair by design**

Audits are conducted by accredited, independent assessors — not chosen or paid directly by the audited party — against a published standard. A **subsidised and peer-verification tier** keeps the Mark within reach of small, open-source, and Global-South builders, so trust is never a privilege of the well-funded.

**How this binds us**

These are commitments to be checked, not admired. The Trust Mark is earned through independent audit and may be withdrawn. Values with teeth — or they are only words.

**An open invitation**

This Accord belongs to no single company or country. Any trustworthy organisation — anywhere, subject to law and to these commitments — that shares these values is invited to join, be audited, and help build. Individuals and institutions may endorse it as supporters.

## Open decisions (next phase)

- **"Open & verifiable": mandatory, tiered, or aspirational?** Decide the tier.
- **Governance pack** (the credibility layer): funding caps + conflict-of-interest policy; independent board/assembly with Global-South & civil-society seats *by rule*; auditor accreditation; appeals/remediation; public audit + revocation history.
- **Security assurance model:** threat model; audit scope (weights/APIs/data/agents/infra/supply chain); audit frequency + continuous monitoring; incident-reporting timelines; transparency logs for signatures/provenance; defence against audit-shopping & "provenance theater" (signing ≠ lawful/consensual data).
- **Go-to-market:** buyers-first (procurement pledges) + a narrow testable v1 (one standard, one governance model, one audit method, one public registry, 3–5 diverse regional pilots; publish pass/fail/revocation early).
- **Positioning:** explicitly complement/extend the Digital Public Goods Alliance's registry model and the Coalition for Secure AI's security standards rather than duplicate them.
