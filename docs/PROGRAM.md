# Program Map

Our AI Charter belongs to a broader program:

> Working to build a free and fair society where technology is used responsibly and enables well-grounded decision-making.

Within that program, **[Our AI Charter](https://github.com/robertschaub/our-ai-charter)** is the public normative framework: purpose, duties, accountability, evaluation, and future assurance structure. This repository is the home of that public normative work.

## Stewardship and governance

Both Our AI Charter and the **[FactHarbor](https://factharbor.ch)** evidence platform are stewarded by the **FactHarbor Verein** — a non-profit association (*Verein*) under Art. 60 ff. of the Swiss Civil Code, based in Dietlikon (Zürich) and founded in 2026. Its statutory purpose is public-benefit: helping people understand, contextualise, and evaluate complex and contested information on an evidence-based foundation.

The two projects pursue that mission from different ends: **FactHarbor** turns contested claims into structured evidence models — the claims, the contexts in which they hold or fail, weighed sources, and traceable verdicts — so reasoning is visible and checkable rather than asserted; **Our AI Charter** sets the accountability standard for the AI systems that increasingly generate such claims.

The Verein binds itself to the same standards this program asks of public AI infrastructure: open-by-default licensing, political and religious neutrality, public disclosure of major funding and of compensation, and a closed list of prohibited funding sources — advertising, sale of user data, pay-for-rating, political or lobbying money, and anything that would compromise its independence. Any surplus is reinvested in the purpose, never distributed.
