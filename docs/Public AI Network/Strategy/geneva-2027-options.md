# Geneva 2027 — contribution routes and partners

How a public-interest **governance + evidence** contribution can reach the **Geneva AI Summit 2027**: the realistic routes in, and the actors who already hold each piece. This consolidates the [initiation strategy](initiation-strategy.md) (sequence), [briefing](../briefing.md) (mission frame and anchors), [actor & landscape map](../Evidence/actors-and-landscape.md) (per-actor detail), and [verified findings](../Evidence/verified-findings.md) (facts and sources) — read those for the underlying detail.

## What the summit is

- The next global AI summit (Bletchley 2023 → Seoul 2024 → Paris 2025 → New Delhi 2026 → **Geneva, first half 2027** → UAE 2028); announced by Federal President Guy Parmelin, 19 Feb 2026.
- **Switzerland hosts**, organised by **UVEK** (parent of BAKOM) + **EDA/FDFA**: project lead **Amb. Markus Reubi** (EDA); content and international representation **Amb. Thomas Schneider** (BAKOM).
- Switzerland positions itself as **neutral convenor / a sectoral "middle way."** Preparation is underway (invite-only roundtable 1 Apr 2026; consultation draft due end-2026).
- Distinct from the annual ITU **AI for Good** Geneva summit. Sources and re-verification dates: [verified findings](../Evidence/verified-findings.md).

## Realistic standing

No slot or role is secured, and this is not a summit-organiser play. The aim is to reach the 2027 window with a small coalition and a **bounded, useful contribution** — host/node, not owner; additive, not competing. The near-term package (defined in the [initiation strategy](initiation-strategy.md)): a neutral pre-sprint, a governance blueprint (co-stewardship + anti-capture), one evidence/evaluation pilot outline (grounding + contestability), and a roundtable path. In the leverage ranking, Geneva 2027 is **"prepare," not "now."**

## Where to start

A relative order by fit and feasibility, not a fixed plan:

1. **Substance first — Route B.** Open public models (Apertus / SNAI) and the Public AI network are the closest fit; engage as an open governance question, not a critique.
2. **Produce the blueprint — Route C.** A neutral pre-sprint turns the idea into a 2-page initiation memo a convenor and a council member can carry.
3. **Open doors — Route D.** International-Geneva conveners supply multilateral framing and visibility.
4. **Then the front door — Route A**, once there is a coalition and one concrete deliverable to show.
5. **Flank later — E / F**, via a carrier council member and public-interest funders.

## Guardrails

Host/node, not owner; additive, not competing with SNAI/Apertus; coalitions claimed as **intent, not fact**; no "Switzerland-first"; capture-resistant against political *and* commercial capture; no certification or audit-authority claim. Full guardrails: [briefing §7](../briefing.md) and the [initiation strategy](initiation-strategy.md). Nothing is booked at the summit — this maps options, it does not assert access.
