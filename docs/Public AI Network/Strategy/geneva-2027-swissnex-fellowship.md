## Why it fits — and why now

The fellowship quietly bundles four things the [routes map](geneva-2027-options.md) treated separately:

- **The side-event path (Routes A/D).** A speaking slot at an *official* summit side event is exactly the "bounded, useful contribution — host/node, not owner; additive, not competing" the strategy targets, pre-arranged.
- **A funded, cohort-based stand-in for the neutral pre-sprint (Route C).** It supplies a forcing deadline, a peer cohort of Swiss thought-leaders, and a platform — and it *requires* the very deliverable already named: the **Geneva 2027 Public AI Governance & Evidence Package** ([initiation strategy](initiation-strategy.md)).
- **Evidence-pillar intelligence.** Direct access to frontier labs is primary input for a credible governance/evaluation blueprint, and strengthens the "network, not fortress" framing.
- **A warm funder line (Route F).** A public-interest relationship with Stiftung Mercator — one of the funders the [verified findings](../Evidence/verified-findings.md) flagged as an open contact question.

In the routes map's ranking, Geneva 2027 is *"prepare, not now."* This fellowship is the vehicle that converts "prepare" into a concrete seat — at low cost and on a near deadline.

## The honest cons

- **Selective** — may not be accepted. → the plan below is built to advance the contribution either way.
- **Real time cost** — a prep day + a full SF week + the Q2 2027 Geneva dates. But that *is* the summit path, not overhead.
- **Anti-capture optics** — a Silicon Valley immersion + a foundation cohort sits slightly against the capture-resistance stance. → manageable: engagement-to-understand ≠ capture, the public output is the independent counterweight, and Mercator is a public-interest, not commercial, funder.
- **Participant, not convener** — seats the contributor as a fellow. → but a side-event mic + a Swiss-thought-leader cohort + a Silicon Valley network is the platform to convene *from*, and it does not preclude the parallel Route C/D convening.

## How to apply — positioning

- **Eligibility does not require shaping policy.** It reads "shape public **discourse *or* policy**," and lists **"AI practitioners"** as its own category — so the contribution qualifies on the discourse + practitioner side.
- **Do not overclaim.** Present the contributor as an **independent AI practitioner and author** — FactHarbor (a public-interest grounding/contestability prototype, alpha) and the Our AI Charter (an open governance spec) — who **contributes to the Swiss public-interest-AI discourse** via published work. Policy work (the [Postulat](../Outreach/postulat.en.md) draft, outreach) goes in as **intent** — "preparing inputs toward 2027" — not achieved influence. Consistent with the no-overclaim guardrail ([briefing §7](../briefing.md)).
- **Name a sharp public output**, not a broad programme: a capture-resistant co-stewardship + grounded-evaluation blueprint, proposed *with* Apertus/SNAI as a governance pilot, not as critique.

## Recommended plan — artifact-first, fellowship-amplified, rejection-proof

**Build one artifact, point it at the summit through the fellowship, and make it rejection-proof.** The keystone is the deliverable to be produced anyway — the **Geneva 2027 Public AI Governance & Evidence Package** (a ~2-page memo). Every route consumes the *same* artifact, so the bet is on one asset, not one channel.

**Why this over the obvious alternatives:**

- *All-in on the fellowship* → single point of failure; selective; arrive empty-handed if cut.
- *Skip it, build a coalition directly (C/D)* → slower, no forcing deadline, and forfeits a pre-arranged official side-event slot + funded SF access.
- *Just wait for the official consultation* → passive, undifferentiated (the April 2026 written-input call drew ~55 submissions), no relationships, no frontier intelligence.

**Sequence:**

0. **Gating decision (the only thing that can kill it):** confirm availability for the three dates — prep day (Sept) · **SF 16–20 Nov 2026** · Geneva side event (Q2 2027). If the SF week is impossible → the fallback.
1. **This week → ~5 Jul:** draft the memo to ~80%; submit the fellowship application built on it (buffer before the **12 Jul** deadline).
2. **In parallel, regardless of outcome:** open *one* substance conversation — the Apertus/SNAI governance question, framed as an open question (Route B), and/or a scoping call with a neutral convener (Expedition Zukunft, Route C).
3. **Mid-August — branch on the result:**
   - **Selected →** the fellowship is the spine: SF immersion sharpens the memo with frontier-lab input; the Q2 2027 side-event slot is the platform; cohort + Mercator line compound.
   - **Not selected →** the memo + a live substance conversation still stand. Route the memo into the official process's next written-input window (consultation draft due end-2026; track via ICT4Peace / Diplo / the Plateforme Tripartite), and pursue a pre-sprint + a parliamentary carrier for the flank.
4. **End-2026:** submit the memo to the official consultation regardless — belt-and-suspenders; a sharp one stands out in a thin field.

**Fallback (no SF week):** drop step 1, keep the memo as keystone, run it through pre-sprint → official consultation → parliamentary carrier — slower and lower-visibility, but fully self-directed.

## Guardrails

Unchanged from the [briefing §7](../briefing.md) and the [routes map](geneva-2027-options.md): host/node, not owner; additive, not competing with SNAI/Apertus; coalitions and processes claimed as **intent, not fact**; capture-resistant against political *and* commercial capture; no certification or audit-authority claim; no "Switzerland-first." Nothing is booked at the summit — this records an opportunity and a recommended path, not secured access.
