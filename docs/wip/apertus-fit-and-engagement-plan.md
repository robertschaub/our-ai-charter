# Apertus — fit, hesitancy & how to engage

*Working notes — 2026-06-29 · WIP · public-safe. Companions: [keystone memo](geneva-2027-keystone-memo.md), [artifact-production plan](artifact-production-plan.md), [initiation strategy](../Strategy/initiation-strategy.md), [non-paper](../Outreach/non-paper.en.md), [actor & landscape map](../Evidence/actors-and-landscape.md), [verified findings](../Evidence/verified-findings.md), [evaluation protocol](../Assurance/Protocol/grounding-faithfulness-and-contestability.md).*

## In one line

The substantive fit is real, but the honest framing is **not** "Our AI Charter adds value to Apertus." It is: **Apertus makes the public-AI governance-and-evidence gap visible; this initiative can contribute one piece of the civic assurance layer — using Apertus as an anchoring case, not as a client — once it has earned standing.** The model team is the wrong first audience; the summit/Metagov/policy ecosystem is the right one.

## Verdict (consolidated)

- **Value-add to the *ecosystem*: strong.** The Geneva 2027 process is explicitly asking for *concrete, reusable tools* (evaluation, audit/transparency, contestability) "beyond declarations," with Apertus named as the flagship open-infra case (see [channel-demand](../Evidence/geneva-2027-channel-demand.md)). The [evaluation protocol](../Assurance/Protocol/grounding-faithfulness-and-contestability.md) nearly answers that call.
- **Value-add to *Apertus the project*: real but latent.** Independent assurance would make its central claim — trustworthiness/transparency — *verifiable* rather than self- or tool-asserted. But a documented gap is **not** the same as the team feeling demand to fill it.
- **Hesitancy: high and predictable** — driven by standing and transaction cost, then by two naming overlaps, not by disagreement on the merits.
- **Match: yes, but complementary and ecosystem-level**, not a model-team partnership. Apertus = model + utility; this initiative = governance + evidence + assurance + convening.

## 1. Does it add value? — yes, but discount two ways

The strongest evidence is external: an independent Swiss analysis ([Geneva Policy Outlook](https://www.genevapolicyoutlook.ch/the-swiss-approach-to-ai-sovereignty/)) praises Apertus's transparency, then names what is missing — third-party assurance of transparency claims, accountability if misuse occurs, democratic oversight beyond institutional development, and coordination with other public-AI initiatives. Those map onto the two building blocks.

Two honest discounts (both reviewers flagged the first as the core error of the first-pass analysis):

1. **Gap ≠ demand ("gap illusion").** A think-tank naming a gap does not mean the *development team* feels urgency. Open-LLM teams prioritise compute, capability, adoption, and shipping; governance reads to them as a compliance tax or an external political problem they expect their own institutions (ETH/EPFL/Confederation) to own. Do not infer team demand from external critique.
2. **The niche is real but narrow and contestable.** Adjacent players could absorb it — Metagov/Public AI (coordination + the inference utility distributing Apertus, 45k+ users; see [actor map](../Evidence/actors-and-landscape.md)), ICAIN (Swiss-led public-interest access), Current AI (international coalition), ISO/EU AI Act (assurance baselines). The defensible niche is the **integrative, capture-resistant assurance-and-evidence layer for public-interest open models, plus the Swiss-host convening logic toward Geneva 2027** — and it is held by execution and coalition, not by documents.

## 2. Would the Apertus team be hesitant? — yes; rank the reasons correctly

1. **Standing + transaction cost (the real #1).** A founder-led, pre-pilot initiative asking busy maintainers of a national flagship for "feedback" is a tax on their time, not a value-add. Warm intros open the door; a weak ask wastes the entry.
2. **Two naming overlaps (a "trust tax").** Apertus already integrates a **"Swiss values charter"** (constitutional-values model alignment — neutrality, linguistic diversity; [swissinfo](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/swiss-ai/fact-and-fiction-about-the-swiss-ai-model-apertus/90110034); details thin) vs. **"Our AI Charter."** And an international **"Public AI Network"** ([publicai.network](https://publicai.network/whitepaper/), Metagov) already distributes Apertus vs. our **"Public AI Network"** building block. Having to spend the first sentence explaining you are *not* their charter / *not* that network signals weak situational awareness or co-optation. See the decision in §5.
3. **Not-invented-here + internal competition.** ETH/EPFL/the Confederation have their own policy apparatus; if they wanted a governance charter for the summit they would likely mandate their own. An external layer competes with internal stakeholders.
4. **Researcher skepticism toward certification** — safetywashing, and "truth can't be certified" (which our own protocol concedes). Lead-heavy certification talk triggers it.
5. **Bandwidth + lane.** Parliamentary/diplomatic work is outside the model team's lane and not their priority.

**Failure modes to design against:** "polite-node purgatory" (a meeting, "interesting," no follow-up — no forcing function); name-confusion backlash; "elite Geneva governance theatre" / Swiss-exceptionalism critique; open-source community suspicion of external governance; and being *too abstract for policymakers, too policy-ish for technologists*.

## 3. Good match? — yes, with the *ecosystem*, not the model team

Values align tightly (transparency, public good, anti-capture, sovereignty-as-network, multilingual inclusion). Roles are complementary, not competing. The hazard is treating "Apertus" as monolithic: the relevant counterparts are **not** the model team but the summit process, Metagov/Public AI, ICAIN, Swiss policy actors, and governance/assurance peers — for whom the contribution is useful even where it is irrelevant to the model engineers. This is consistent with the initiative's existing discipline: *use Apertus/SNAI as pilot anchor, not the whole frame; host/node/bridge, not owner; raise as a question, not criticism* ([initiation strategy](../Strategy/initiation-strategy.md)).

## 4. The reframe (the spine of any pitch)

Replace "we add value to Apertus" (extractive, presumptuous) with: *Apertus makes a broader public-AI governance/evidence gap visible; we are developing one concrete piece of the civic assurance layer for public-interest open AI, using Apertus as a motivating case where appropriate — no endorsement sought.* This keeps the mission frame (a free and fair society in the digital space; **sovereignty not autarky**) and avoids national-branding Apertus without standing.

## 5. Decision needed — the two names

Both reviewers rate the overlaps **serious** and recommend renaming/subordinating; neither knew the names may be deliberate. The genuine question for the maintainer:

- **Embrace (membership signal):** keep "Public AI Network," positioning the work as a *contribution into* the existing Metagov movement (the governance/evidence + sovereignty/resilience + Swiss-node track). Honest only if that relationship is real and acknowledged by them; otherwise it reads as appropriation.
- **Subordinate (recommended default):** in Apertus-/summit-facing material, lead with **functional** names — "public-AI governance & evidence layer," "civic assurance protocol" — and keep "Our AI Charter" / "Public AI Network" as background umbrella terms, with a one-line disclaimer distinguishing both from Apertus's values charter and from publicai.network.
- **Rename:** retire the colliding terms for strictly functional ones.

*This is a public-facing framing change — a doc-guard hard stop — so it is flagged here for decision, not actioned.* Recommendation: **subordinate now**, decide embrace-vs-rename once the Metagov/Public AI relationship is tested (see plan).

## 6. Plan — earn standing first, approach the model team last

Sharpens the Apertus angle *within* the existing Geneva-2027 plan; does not replace it. Lead move is an **unprompted proof-of-concept**: bring results, not a framework.

**Now (0–30 days) — make the asset, not the ask.**
- Run the [grounding-faithfulness & contestability protocol](../Assurance/Protocol/grounding-faithfulness-and-contestability.md) on **one** open public model on one use-case (cited-source Q&A). Apertus is open — no permission needed to *evaluate* it — but **name Apertus publicly only with partner agreement** ([production-plan discipline](artifact-production-plan.md)); otherwise report it as "an Apertus-class open public model."
- Draft a short **discussion note** — "Assurance & governance questions for public-interest open AI (a Geneva 2027 note)": problem space, 3–5 concrete questions, the one-line distinction from the two namesakes. Reuse the [keystone memo](geneva-2027-keystone-memo.md); do not write a new manifesto.

**Next (31–90 days) — earn standing outside the model team.**
- Circulate the note + PoC results to **3–5 credible governance/assurance/public-interest reviewers** for correction (not endorsement).
- Test the **Metagov / Public AI** relationship directly (Joshua Tan is the connective node — Public AI utility, Metagov, interim Current AI): is the work a welcome contribution *into* the movement, or a parallel? Their answer resolves §5.
- Use the **summit process** (where Apertus is in scope) and ICAIN (Katharina Frey — an existing Swiss co-stewardship instance) as the venues, not the model team.

**Then (3–6 months) — approach Apertus-adjacent contacts, with results and a modest ask.**
- Only after standing exists: a low-burden, *correcting* ask to a governance-minded Apertus-orbit contact (e.g. Menna El-Assady) — "here is how an open public model scores on independent process-assurance; what would be unhelpful or misleading?" — **not** a cold governance proposal (there is no public intake; route via summit + warm peers).
- Keep the first ask extremely cheap to grant.

## 7. Verify before relying externally

Repo-verified already (cite from [verified findings](../Evidence/verified-findings.md) / [actor map](../Evidence/actors-and-landscape.md)): Apertus = EPFL/ETH/CSCS, Apache-2.0, released 2 Sept 2025; Tan/Public AI distributes Apertus; governance publicly lean; summit names Apertus + asks for tools-not-declarations; no public governance intake.

Still to verify / treat as open: whether the Geneva Policy Outlook gaps reflect any *internal* Apertus priority (vs. external commentary — assume not); the exact scope/governance of the "Swiss values charter"; whether named warm peers (Frey, El-Assady) are structurally able to carry a third-party governance/evidence model toward Apertus, or are siloed; any Swisscom commercial/policy sensitivities around naming.
