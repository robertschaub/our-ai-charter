# Assurance case — *Fair in practice* (obligation 4)

*A Claims → Arguments → Evidence view; status **derived** from duty/module findings, never scored on its own. A view over the duties, not a new control layer.*

## Claim

Where the system may materially affect people, its performance is **tested and monitored for uneven or discriminatory outcomes** across affected groups, languages, regions, and contexts — and known disparities are **disclosed, mitigated, constrained, or withdrawn.**

## Scope & trigger

**Conditional** — applies only where a system may materially affect people, rights, opportunities, or access to services. Inherently **collective**: assessed on aggregates, not a single output. Tier and which groups/contexts to test are a **risk-calibrator** call.

## Argument & evidence — the crosswalk

| Duty (argument) | What it must show | Evidence / module | Min. depth (high tier) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D6 — fairness, inclusion, oversight, remedy** *(primary)* | affected-group identification; disparate failure/refusal/error testing across groups, languages, regions, contexts, accessibility; mitigation | fairness module | **effectiveness-tested** |
| **D3 — risk register** | disparate-impact risk registered and tiered | risk register | evidence-observed |
| **D7 — transparency, limits** | known disparities and limits disclosed | public limitations statement | evidence-observed |
| **D8 — monitoring, drift** | ongoing disparity monitoring; drift checks | monitoring records | implementation-checked |

## Remedy & escalation (derived)

**Systemic, not individual** — mitigate / re-train / constrain / withdraw the affected scope, and disclose. Individual instances of unfair treatment route to *[Answerable](answerable-to-people-assurance-case.md)*; the affected groups are declared in *[Purpose-bound](purpose-bound-assurance-case.md)*.

## Failure / withdrawal triggers

Untested where disparate impact is plausible; a material disparity hidden; mitigation absent where a disparity is known. → withdraw or narrow.

## Derived status & claim discipline

Weakest primary row (D6). *Allowed*: "tested for disparate error across [named groups]; implementation-checked." *Not allowed*: "the system is fair / unbiased." Where measurement is genuinely impossible, the **gap is disclosed, not waived** (burden does not shift to silence).

## What this does not prove

Not fairness-of-outcome in any absolute sense — only that disparities were measured, disclosed, and mitigated to the stated depth on the assessed groups.

## What this prototype tested (finding)

**This is where the collective-impact lens and the risk calibrator do the most work** — evidence is statistical/aggregate, remedy is systemic, and "which groups and contexts count" is a calibrator judgment. It confirms those two lenses are load-bearing, not decorative. It is also the **most conditional** obligation (it only fires where material impact is plausible), so its *scope & trigger* row carries unusual weight — and it interlocks tightly: groups declared in Purpose-bound, individual redress in Answerable, monitoring shared with obligations 3 and 5.
