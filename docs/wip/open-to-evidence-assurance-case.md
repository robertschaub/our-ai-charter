# Assurance case — *Open to evidence & correction* (obligation 5)

*A Claims → Arguments → Evidence view; status **derived** from duty/module findings, never scored on its own. This obligation is also where **honesty-of-process** (warranted public claims, across all obligations) is enforced — and it governs the integrity of the other cards' own claims (see the finding).*

## Claim

Material claims, decisions, and risk-relevant behaviour **leave a trail others can check**: sources shown where used; uncertainty and limits signalled; errors corrected and **not regenerated** — and the system's own public claims are **warranted**.

## Scope & trigger

Any system making checkable claims or consequential decisions. **Honesty-of-process applies across all five obligations**; this is where it is enforced, and where the factual-output **grounding-faithfulness** module lives.

## Argument & evidence — the crosswalk

| Duty (argument) | What it must show | Evidence / module | Min. depth (high tier) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D7 — transparency, provenance, claim-integrity** *(primary)* | claim↔source traceability; uncertainty/limits signalled; AI-involvement notice; generated-content marking; unsupported claims corrected | grounding-faithfulness module | **effectiveness-tested** (factual systems) |
| **D5 — data governance, provenance** | training/retrieval/data-source provenance retained | privacy/provenance module | evidence-observed |
| **D8 — correction, change, incidents** | correction records; paraphrase-regression; drift/currentness; withdrawal of false claims | correction records | implementation-checked |
| **D3 — residual-risk disclosure** | unresolved risks and limits disclosed in the release assessment | release risk assessment | evidence-observed |

## Remedy & escalation (derived)

Correct → re-verify (paraphrase-regression) → **withdraw the claim** if it cannot be corrected. This is the remedy the *other* cards borrow — their "withdraw on false evidence / curated logs" triggers are obligation 5 operating.

## Failure / withdrawal triggers

A fabricated or unresolvable citation; curated correction logs; a silent version swap; a capability/marketing overclaim; an over-redacted release assessment. → withdraw.

## Derived status & claim discipline

Weakest primary row (D7). **And the meta-twist:** the derived-status + claim-discipline rule that appears in *every* card — "say only what the assurance depth supports" — **is this obligation operating reflexively** on the assurance claims themselves.

## What this does not prove

Not truth, and not source *reliability* — only that the evidentiary relation (claim↔cited source), the uncertainty signalling, and the correction process work to the stated depth.

## What this prototype tested (finding)

**Obligation 5 is partly meta.** It is the **module-heavy** counterpart to the duty-heavy *Purpose-bound* (it leans on grounding-faithfulness) — *and* it governs the integrity of the assurance claims the other four cards make. This is the strongest validation of the earlier reframe: honesty-of-process belongs **across** the spine (enforced here, with grounding as one module), and the claim-discipline rule used in all five cards is simply this obligation applied to the Charter's own outputs. It closes the loop.
